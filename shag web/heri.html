HERITAGE OF NAWABS

CHIKANKARI

the fabric of nawabs

Chikankari is a traditional and intricate form of hand embroidery that originated in Lucknow, India. This exquisite art form has a rich history and is known for its delicate and artistic patterns. Here is a paragraph on Chikankari in Lucknow:

Chikankari, a timeless and elegant form of hand embroidery, finds its roots in the culturally rich city of Lucknow. This traditional craft is synonymous with the heritage of the region and has become a symbol of Lucknawi artistic finesse. The art of Chikankari involves the delicate and skilled hand embroidery of various patterns on fabric, typically cotton, muslin, or silk. The craftsmen, known as "karigars," meticulously create floral, paisley, and geometric designs using a variety of stitches. The elegance and sophistication of Chikankari have made it a sought-after choice for ethnic wear, including sarees, salwar suits, and dupattas. Beyond its aesthetic appeal, Chikankari represents a cultural legacy passed down through generations, making it an integral part of Lucknow's identity and a beloved craft appreciated globally.

ZARDOZI

from the littlest streets to the magzines

Zardozi, an opulent and intricate form of metal embroidery, has its roots deeply embedded in the cultural heritage of Lucknow, India. This ancient craft, which means "gold embroidery" in Persian, showcases the exceptional craftsmanship and artistic prowess of the skilled artisans of Lucknow. Here is a brief paragraph on Zardozi in Lucknow:

Zardozi, the art of embellishing fabrics with metallic threads and beads, has been a hallmark of Lucknow's cultural tapestry for centuries. The city's skilled craftsmen, known as "zardoz," meticulously create stunning designs on various fabrics, including silk, velvet, and brocade, using gold and silver threads along with precious stones, pearls, and sequins. The result is a dazzling tapestry of intricate patterns, ranging from floral motifs to geometric shapes, that adorns traditional garments like sarees, lehengas, and sherwanis. Zardozi, with its royal allure and regal charm, has not only become a symbol of luxury but also a testament to the rich artistic heritage of Lucknow. This traditional craft continues to thrive, bridging the gap between the past and the present and contributing to the city's reputation as a hub of exquisite craftsmanship.
